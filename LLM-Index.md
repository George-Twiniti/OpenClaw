## Key Definitions

### Digital Twin
**Definition:** A digital representation of a physical asset, system, or environment used to connect design, data, and operations.

**Source:** /AI/2022-12-19_Digital_Engineering

### BIM
**Definition:** A structured approach to managing built-environment information across design, construction, and operations.

**Source:** /AI/2024-03-15_BIM_Protocol_Overview

### Metadata
**Definition:** Structured information that describes, classifies, and helps govern content and assets.

**Source:** /AI/2025-09-22_Metadata_in_AI_systems

### Master Data Management
**Definition:** A governance layer for maintaining consistent, trusted enterprise data across systems and use cases.

**Source:** /AI/2025-09-22_Metadata_in_AI_systems

### RAG
**Definition:** Retrieval-augmented generation, a pattern that combines retrieval with model generation.

**Source:** /AI/2025-09-22_Metadata_in_AI_systems

## Questions

### Is metadata dead in a world of AI?
**Answer:** No. Metadata is still important for filtering, governance, trust, and auditability, even when semantic search can find content without perfect tagging.

**Source:** /AI/2025-09-22_Metadata_in_AI_systems

### How does RAG use metadata?
**Answer:** Metadata helps narrow the search space, improves precision, and supports hybrid retrieval where semantic search is combined with structured filters.

**Source:** /AI/2025-09-22_Metadata_in_AI_systems

### Why does metadata matter in document management?
**Answer:** It supports classification, retention, compliance, and faster retrieval, especially in large repositories with mixed structured and unstructured content.

**Source:** /AI/2025-09-22_Metadata_in_AI_systems
