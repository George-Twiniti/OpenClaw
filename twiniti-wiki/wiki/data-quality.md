# Data quality and stewardship

Data quality is the measurable fitness of data for use. Stewardship is the human and operational discipline that keeps it fit.

## Common quality dimensions

- completeness
- validity
- consistency
- timeliness
- uniqueness
- accuracy where verifiable

## Stewardship responsibilities

- monitor issues
- resolve exceptions
- approve changes
- reconcile conflicts
- define escalation paths

## Where quality should be checked

- at data capture
- during integration
- before publication
- before high-risk decisions

## Practical approach

Focus on the handful of fields that drive decisions, not every field equally.

## Failure pattern

If quality is only checked after data lands in analytics, the organization is already paying for bad data twice: once to create it, and again to clean it up.
