# Ontology and asset semantics

Ontology gives structure to meaning: it defines how assets, parts, locations, events, and attributes relate to one another.

## Why semantics matter

Two systems can use the same field name and still mean different things. Ontology reduces ambiguity by standardizing meaning, not just syntax.

## What to represent

- asset types and subtypes
- part-whole relationships
- containment and location
- events and states
- measurements and thresholds
- work, failure, and inspection concepts

## Practical uses

- more reliable integration
- better search and discovery
- consistent analytics
- safer AI use on top of governed data

## Keep it practical

An ontology should be:
- understandable by domain experts
- implementable in software
- aligned to the canonical asset model
- small enough to maintain

## Common mistake

Starting with an elaborate ontology before the operational data foundation exists. In practice, useful semantics evolve with the implementation.
