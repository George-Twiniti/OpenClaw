# What is a Digital Twin?

A digital twin is a living digital representation of a physical asset, system, or environment that stays connected to lifecycle data and operational context.

## What makes it a twin

A useful digital twin is more than a 3D model or dashboard. It usually includes:
- an asset identity
- relationships to other assets and systems
- current and historical state
- context from design, maintenance, sensors, and operations
- decision support or simulation capability

## Core value

Digital twins help organizations:
- understand what exists and how it behaves
- monitor condition and performance
- simulate options before acting
- connect capital, operations, and maintenance decisions
- preserve knowledge across the asset lifecycle

## Levels of maturity

1. **Static representation** — a model or drawing with limited context.
2. **Connected view** — data linked from multiple systems.
3. **Operational twin** — near-real-time or regularly refreshed state.
4. **Predictive twin** — analytics and simulation support decisions.
5. **Closed-loop twin** — insights trigger workflows or controls.

## Common misconceptions

- A twin is not just a 3D visualization.
- Real-time data is useful, but not required for every twin.
- If identity and semantics are weak, the twin becomes a pretty integration layer rather than a reliable system.

## Relationship to EAM

EAM manages assets operationally; the digital twin provides richer context and decision support across the lifecycle. In practice, the twin often depends on governed asset data from the EAM foundation.
