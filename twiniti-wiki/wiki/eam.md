# What is Enterprise Asset Management?

Enterprise Asset Management (EAM) is the discipline of planning, maintaining, tracking, and optimizing physical assets across their lifecycle.

## What it covers

EAM typically includes:
- asset register and hierarchy management
- maintenance planning and work execution
- inspections, reliability, and condition tracking
- capital planning and lifecycle costing
- spare parts and inventory coordination
- compliance, safety, and audit evidence

## Why it matters

Asset-intensive organizations depend on EAM to:
- reduce downtime
- extend asset life
- improve safety and compliance
- prioritize capital spending
- coordinate engineering, operations, and maintenance

## EAM as a system of record

EAM is often the operational backbone for:
- asset identity
- location and hierarchy
- work orders and maintenance history
- criticality and performance metrics

But EAM is rarely the only source of truth. Asset data is usually distributed across BIM, GIS, CMMS, ERP, IoT, document systems, and engineering repositories.

## Common failure modes

- duplicate asset identities
- inconsistent naming and hierarchy rules
- maintenance data disconnected from design context
- poor handover from project to operations
- weak ownership for master data updates

## Practical guidance

A strong EAM foundation needs:
- a canonical asset model
- governed identifiers
- clear ownership by data domain
- lifecycle-linked metadata
- integration with design, operations, and analytics systems
