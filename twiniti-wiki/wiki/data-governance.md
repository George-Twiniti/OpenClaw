# Data governance foundations

Data governance is the set of roles, rules, controls, and workflows that keep data trusted, usable, and accountable.

## In asset-intensive organizations

Governance must cover more than reporting and compliance. It needs to shape how asset data is:
- defined
- created
- approved
- changed
- integrated
- consumed

## Foundational principles

- **Ownership** — every critical data domain has an accountable owner.
- **Definitions** — terms and attributes have explicit meaning.
- **Standards** — naming, coding, hierarchy, and classification follow rules.
- **Lineage** — important data can be traced back to its source.
- **Quality** — completeness, validity, timeliness, and consistency are measured.
- **Access control** — users only see and change what they should.
- **Change management** — updates are reviewed and versioned.

## Operational governance vs advisory governance

Advisory governance documents issues. Operational governance prevents them.

Operational governance is embedded in:
- source system workflows
- integration pipelines
- validation rules
- steward approvals
- audit logs

## What to govern first

Start with data that affects:
- safety
- regulatory compliance
- maintenance execution
- capital decisions
- operational reporting

## Failure patterns

- governance treated as a committee rather than a workflow
- definitions stored in documents but not enforced in systems
- stewardship unclear or symbolic
- quality issues detected downstream instead of at entry
